Mushroom Consommé | DNATA:021 CAT:M14246 

Ingredients
mixed mushrooms (champignon, porcini, cremini) 1 kg
onion, finely chopped 200 g
celery, finely chopped 100 g
carrot, finely chopped 100 g
egg whites 100 g
salt 5 g
black peppercorns 3 g
vegetable broth 2 l
thyme 2 sprigs
bay leaf 2 leaves
Method
Clean and finely chop the mushrooms.
In a pot, sauté the onion, celery, and carrot until golden.
Add the mushrooms and cook for about 10 minutes.
Pour in the vegetable broth, add thyme, bay leaf, salt, and black peppercorns.
Bring to a boil, then lower the heat and simmer for 30 minutes.
In a bowl, lightly whisk the egg whites and pour them into the broth.
Let cook on low heat without stirring; the egg whites will form a “raft” that clarifies the broth.
Strain the consommé through a fine cloth or cheesecloth and keep warm.

Milk Onion Soup | DNATA:022 CAT:M14847 

Ingredients
Onions 3,500 g (thinly sliced)
Butter 500 g
All-purpose flour 350 g
Whole milk 8,000 g
Water 1,500 g
Salt 40 g (adjust to taste)
Black pepper 10 g (freshly ground)
Bay leaves 10 g
Thyme 5 g
Nutmeg 5 g
Method
Sauté the onions: In a large pot, melt the butter over medium heat. Add the sliced onions and sauté for about 20-30 minutes, stirring occasionally, until they are soft and golden brown.
Add the flour: Once the onions are caramelized, sprinkle the flour over the onions and stir well. Cook for about 5 minutes to get rid of the raw flour taste, stirring continuously to prevent lumps.
Incorporate the milk and water: Gradually add the whole milk (8,000 g) and water (1,500 g), stirring constantly to ensure the mixture is smooth. Bring the soup to a simmer over medium heat, stirring frequently.
Season: Add the salt, black pepper, bay leaves, thyme, and nutmeg. Stir well to combine. Simmer the soup for 30-40 minutes, allowing the flavors to meld together. Stir occasionally to prevent the soup from sticking to the bottom of the pot.
Adjust the seasoning: Taste the soup and adjust the salt and pepper to your liking.

Ingredients
Arborio rice 1.8 kg
Vegetable stock 4.5 L
White onion, finely chopped 200 g
Smoked tofu, crumbled 300 g
Olive oil 100 ml
Saffron threads 2 g
Salt 15 g
Black pepper 3 g
Method
Heat the vegetable stock and keep it warm.
In a large pan, heat olive oil and sauté the finely chopped onion until soft.
Add the Arborio rice and toast for 2 minutes, stirring constantly.
Gradually add the warm vegetable stock, stirring continuously until the rice is al dente.
Dissolve the saffron threads in a small amount of warm stock and incorporate them into the risotto.
Season with salt, black pepper and add the tofu.

Jeera Rice | DNATA:113 CAT:M10414 

Ingredients:
Basmati rice (soaked 30 minutes, drained): 1000 g
Ghee or vegetable oil: 80 g
Cumin seeds (jeera): 12 g
Bay leaf: 1 pc
Green cardamom pods (lightly crushed): 2 pcs
Cloves: 2 pcs
Water: 1.6 L
Salt: 18 g
Method:
Rinse and soak the basmati rice for 30 minutes, then drain thoroughly.
In a wide saucepan or heavy pot, heat the ghee over medium heat. Add cumin seeds, bay leaf, cardamom, and cloves.
Sauté the spices for 30–60 seconds, until fragrant and the cumin begins to crackle.
Add the drained rice and sauté gently for 1–2 minutes, coating each grain in the spiced ghee.
Add water and salt, bring to a boil, then cover and reduce heat to low.
Simmer for 15–18 minutes, or until rice is tender and water is absorbed.
Remove from heat and rest covered for 5–10 minutes, then fluff gently with a fork.

Black pepper (ground): 2 g
Fresh basil or parsley (chopped): 10 g
Lemon zest (optional): 2 g

Lavash Cracker Bread with Mint and Cumin | DNATA:117 CAT:M25110 

Ingredients (for approx. 800 g dough):
All-purpose flour: 500 g
Water: 200 g
Olive oil: 50 g
Salt: 10 g
Ground cumin: 4 g
Dried mint (crumbled): 6 g
Sugar: 5 g
Instant dry yeast (optional for lightness): 2 g
Method:
In a mixing bowl, combine flour, salt, sugar, cumin, and dried mint.
In a separate bowl, mix water, olive oil, and yeast (if using).
Add wet ingredients to the dry and knead into a smooth, elastic dough — about 6–8 minutes by hand or 3–4 minutes in a mixer.
Wrap and rest the dough at room temperature for 30 minutes.
Preheat oven to 200–220°C. Line trays with parchment or use a baking stone.
Divide dough into small pieces. Roll each one very thinly (1–2 mm) into rectangles or free-form shapes.
Transfer to trays, brush lightly with olive oil, and sprinkle with extra mint, cumin, or salt if desired.
Bake for 6–10 minutes, or until crisp and lightly golden. Watch closely to prevent burning.
Cool on a rack. Store in an airtight container to maintain crispness.

Ingredients
Polenta (cornmeal) 1.2 kg
Water 4 L
Salt 30 g
Olive oil 100 ml
Fresh rosemary (finely chopped) 10 g
Parmesan cheese (grated) 200 g
Butter 150 g
Method
Bring water to a boil in a large pot and add salt.
Gradually whisk in the polenta and stir continuously for 40 minutes over low heat.

Once set, cut into portions and pan-fry in olive oil over medium heat until golden brown on both sides.
